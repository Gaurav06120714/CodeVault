## 1. CodeVault "Flash Arenas" (The Addictive Multiplayer Feature)

**The Real-World Problem:** Practicing for DSA rounds is incredibly lonely and boring. Standard
contests (like LeetCode Weekly) are intimidating, 2 hours long, and only happen on weekends.

**The Unique Feature:** You and 3 friends from your college are in a WhatsApp group. You go to
CodeVault and click **"Create Flash Arena."** CodeVault instantly cross-references all 4 of your
accounts, finds 2 problems across LeetCode or CodeChef that **none of you have ever solved before**,
and generates a private room. You all hit **"Start."** When you open LeetCode, the CodeVault
browser extension injects a **Live Multiplayer Scoreboard** directly into your LeetCode screen. As
you are typing your code, you can see a progress bar showing that your friend just passed 3 test
cases.

**Why it's addictive:** It turns boring solo practice into an adrenaline-filled, 15-minute
multiplayer game with your actual friends. It completely gamifies DSA prep.

**Why it's patentable & profitable:** You can patent the method of dynamically generating "unseen"
problem sets by cross-referencing multiple users' cross-platform database histories.

**Business Model:** You can sell "CodeVault Arena" to colleges or coding clubs (like ASCEND) so they
can host automated, live-tracked micro-tournaments for their students.
